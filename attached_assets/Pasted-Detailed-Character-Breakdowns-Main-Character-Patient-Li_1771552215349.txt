Detailed Character Breakdowns

Main Character (Patient: "Liam")

Appearance: Short, straight dark brown hair, slightly damp and messy from sweat.

Condition (Positioning): Sitting rigidly upright in the "tripod position" (leaning forward, hands braced firmly on his knees or the edge of a sofa). He cannot deviate from this position due to severe distress.
Condition (Respiratory): Severe respiratory distress (dyspnea). Rapid, shallow breathing (tachypnea). Audible, high-pitched expiratory wheezing. Pronounced intercostal and substernal retractions (skin pulling tightly between the ribs and below the sternum on inhalation), clearly visible at the neckline and beneath the T-shirt. Dramatic nasal flaring (nostrils widening significantly) with every breath.
Condition (Circulation/Skin): Skin is distinctly pale with a grayish tint (pallor). Cool and clammy appearance, with a visible sheen of sweat (diaphoresis) on the forehead and upper lip. Lips may have a slight bluish tint (cyanosis).
Demeanor: Eyes wide with profound anxiety and fatigue. Alert and tracking movement, but completely unable to speak due to severe breathlessness.

Environment, Lighting, and Style Guidelines
Style: 8K Photorealistic, 16:9 aspect ratio. Cinematic realism, simulating the look of an Arri Alexa camera with a 50mm prime lens. Steady, documentary-style camera work prioritizing clinical detail.
Environment: Starts in a clean, middle-class suburban living room (neutral colors, beige walls, hardwood floor, gray sofa). Transitions toward the exit and then the ambulance interior.
Lighting (Living Room): Bright, soft, natural daylight from a large window as the key light. High-key lighting, ensuring even illumination with no harsh shadows, allowing clear visualization of the patient's pallor and retractions.
Lighting (Ambulance): Bright, cool-white (5000K) overhead LED lighting. Clinical and sterile appearance.

Negative Prompt (Apply to all): text, logos, gore, blood, brand names, distortions, exaggerated or cartoonish facial expressions, jewelry, tattoos, low resolution, blurry images, visible camera equipment, incorrect medical procedures, incorrect anatomy, extra limbs, missing fingers, flickering lights, fire, smoke, environmental hazards.
